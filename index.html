About the Project

The Deliberation Knowledge Graph project aims to connect various deliberative process datasets and ontologies into a comprehensive knowledge graph. This project provides a unified framework for analyzing deliberative processes across different platforms and contexts, from formal parliamentary debates to citizen participation initiatives.

By integrating diverse datasets and ontologies, the Deliberation Knowledge Graph enables researchers, policymakers, and citizens to explore the connections between different deliberative processes, identify patterns, and gain insights into how deliberation works across various contexts.

Datasets

The project integrates several datasets related to deliberative processes from various sources:

Decide Madrid

Citizen proposals and comments from Madrid's participatory democracy platform, enabling citizens to propose, debate, and vote on city initiatives.

Ontologies

The Deliberation Knowledge Graph integrates and extends several existing ontologies. Below is a table of relevant ontologies, inspired by the approach used in the Lynx Project. These ontologies help model the structures, roles, and relationships involved in deliberative processes, argumentation, participation, and legal contexts.

Ontology Available (rdf | doc) Name Description Language Jurisdiction
aif rdf
doc		 Argument Interchange Format A core ontology designed to represent and exchange argument structures, enabling interoperability between argumentation tools. Developed to capture premises, conclusions, and relations in structured debate. en all
arg-ont rdf
doc		 Argument Ontology Provides common concepts and relationships to model argumentation entities (claims, reasons, evidence) and how they interrelate, supporting structured debate and reasoning. en all
sioc-arg rdf
doc		 SIOC Argumentation An extension of the SIOC initiative that represents argumentation structures in online community discussions, enabling semantic linking of posts, replies, and arguments. en all
ibis rdf
doc		 IBIS (Issue-Based Information System) A method for modeling issues, ideas, and arguments in a structured format, widely used for deliberative problem-solving and debate mapping. en all
delib rdf
doc		 DELIB Ontology Geared to capture essential elements of deliberative processes and participant interactions, emphasizing how contributions, interventions, and outcomes connect within a deliberation. en all
deliberation-ont rdf
doc		 Deliberation Ontology Focuses on dialogues for collaborative decision-making. Represents claims, proposals, and reasoning steps to support structured deliberative processes and track outcomes. en all
participation rdf
doc		 Participation Ontology Models citizen and stakeholder involvement in decision-making, from contributions and arguments to roles, events, and procedures in e-participation contexts. en all
consensus rdf
doc		 Consensus Ontology Represents how participants converge on agreements or decisions through deliberative processes, emphasizing consensus-building mechanisms. en all
lkif-core rdf
doc		 LKIF-Core A legal ontology consisting of modules that describe concepts from the legal and commonsense domains (e.g., legal roles, norms, and procedures), aiding in structured representation of legal knowledge. en eu
